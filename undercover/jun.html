The Beginning: Tokyo

Jun Takahashi grew up in the village of Kiryu. From a very young age, he was always interested in fashion, enrolling at the Bunka college of Fashion in 1988. Punk Rock influence quickly swept Takahashi up and inspired him to participate in the Tokyo Sex Pistols, a tribute band to the English counterparts. Following this youthful inspiration, Takahashi found himself in the small district of Urahara.

Jun 'Junio' Takahashi (left) and Tomoaki 'Nigo' Nagao (right), 1995.

In 1990, while studying at the Bunka College of Fashion, Takahashi met Nigo. Together, they opened up a small boutique called Nowhere. The store served as a home for both Takahashi’s Undercover and Nigo’s Bape. The first Undercover runway show took place in 1994 in Tokyo. For the next several years, Takahashi continued to produce seasonal womenswear runway shows in Tokyo with funding from maintaining steady business with Nigo at Nowhere.

Nowhere Store Front, 1993

The Paris Spotlight

Once an influence for Takahashi, Rei Kawakubo of Comme des Garçon began to promote Undercover to her Paris colleagues. Undercover’s first show in Paris for Spring/Summer ’03 “Scab” went on to become arguably the greatest Undercover collection to date. With this work, Takahashi was propelled into the global fashion spotlight and reached stock lists across the globe (Rotman; Li).

From here on out, Undercover continued to show seasonally in Paris. Takahashi’s signature grunge aesthetic and unconventional formats continued to evolve for the next several years. As Undercover grew in popularity, the streetwear punk influence of Japan evolved into a more contemporary style for Takahashi. Modern Undercover continues to mix pop-culture streetwear influence with high-end cuts and proportions, but as the punk influence has faded away, collections have become more accessible and less niche.
